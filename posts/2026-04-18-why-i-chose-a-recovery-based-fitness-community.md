When I first started thinking about a direction for this project, I knew I did not want to design a general fitness platform. There are already many apps and communities built around training, performance, and appearance. What interested me more was a group of users whose needs often sit in between fitness and health, but are not fully supported by either.

I chose to focus on injury recovery because I am currently going through that process myself. While trying to return to training, I noticed that a lot of existing fitness platforms assume users are ready to push forward, improve quickly, or follow standard programs. That mindset does not match the uncertainty of recovery. At this stage, users are often not looking for motivation or intensity. They are looking for clarity, reassurance, and relevance.

A lot of the questions I found myself asking were very specific. Is this exercise safe at my current stage? Is this level of pain normal, or is it a sign that I should stop? What are other people doing when they are at a similar point in recovery? These are practical questions, but they are difficult to answer through generic fitness communities. Most of the content is too broad, mixed together, or centred on people with very different goals.

This made me realise there is a gap between existing fitness platforms and what recovery users actually need. The problem is not simply a lack of content. It is also a lack of structure. When someone is recovering, timing and context matter. Advice that is useful in one phase may be unhelpful or unsafe in another. Because of that, a recovery-focused platform needs to organise information differently from a normal fitness app.

My personal experience does not automatically make the project correct, but it does give me a clearer starting point. I have a better sense of what kind of information feels useful, what kind of uncertainty users experience, and why trust matters so much in this context. That makes this direction feel more grounded than if I had chosen a topic I only understood from a distance.

For that reason, I decided to focus this project on a recovery-based community rather than a general fitness platform. It feels more specific, more meaningful, and more aligned with the kind of user problem I want to explore in this course.
